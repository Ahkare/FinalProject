The legend of Zelda games feature a mixture of puzzles, action, adventure/battle gameplay, and exploration. These elements have remained constant throughout the series, but with refinements and additions featured in each new game. Later games in the series also include stealth gameplay, where the player must avoid enemies while proceeding through a level, as well as racing elements.

Although the games can be beaten with a minimal amount of exploration and side quests, the player is frequently rewarded with helpful items or increased abilities for solving puzzles or exploring hidden areas. Some items are consistent and appear many times throughout the series , while others are unique to a single game. Though the games contain many role-playing elements, they emphasize straightforward hack and slash-style combat over the strategic, turn-based combat of series' like Final Fantasy. The game's role-playing elements, however, have led to much debate over whether or not the Zelda games should be classified as action role-playing games, a genre on which the series has had a strong influence.

On top of the classic RPG's and Mainline zelda games, spin-offs (games featuring the universe of the game but aren't part of the main series) came to exist, giving the fans of the series a plethora of different settings to explore the world in.

The latest entry in the series transported the player into a devastated world full of unknown technology 100 years after the Hero lost in a battle against Calamity Ganon.

Overall, as you can see, the zelda universe is huge and rich in personality, with timelines diverging into multiple eras. going from a classic RPG to tactical games, action games and most recently : Rythm, This universe will not stop growing, hope to see you trying one out !
